We are interested in your views about job creation. A commission that oversees a federal annual fund has developed plans for the number of temporary (1-year contract) jobs that can be created in the next few years. This annual fund is available starting this year until two years later and will be used for job creation as well as an unrelated different federal project. We want to know how you would allocate new jobs between two years. It is possible to create all jobs in the earlier year or all jobs in the later year. It is also possible to split the number of jobs created between the two years.

The commission considered three different plans for each of the following three programs:

    Program 1: Create new jobs either this year or one year later, while using the funds for the unrelated federal project two years later. Program 2: Create new jobs either this year or two years later, while pausing job creation next year using the funds for the unrelated federal project. Program 3: Create new jobs either one year later or two years later, while using the funds for the unrelated federal project this year.


For each program, you will be asked to make 6 choices, for a total of 18 choices over the future plans about job creation. Each of the 18 choices will be over how to best allocate the temporary jobs between two points in time. In other words, you will be asked to choose which combination of jobs created between the earlier year and the later year you think is best to implement. Please make each choice for each program as if it is the plan that will be implemented. Remember there are no “right” or “wrong” answers.
