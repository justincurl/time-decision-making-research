FUTURE: {{curr_block.text_delay_start}} and {{curr_block.text_total_end}}


The next section of this survey is still about temporary job creation now, a year from now, and five years from now. In this section, you will use a different way to indicate which combination of temporary jobs created in two periods you think represents the best economy.

First, we will ask you several questions about temporary (1-year contract) jobs created {{curr_block.text_delay_start}} and temporary jobs created {{curr_block.text_total_end}}.

For each question, you will see 6 options of how to distribute jobs across periods. The leftmost response button indicates that all 500,000 jobs are created {{curr_block.text_delay_start}}. The rightmost response button indicates that all jobs are created {{curr_block.text_total_end}}. The buttons in between represent different combinations of some jobs created {{curr_block.text_delay_start}} and some jobs created {{curr_block.text_total_end}}. So, you decide how to distribute jobs created between the two periods to represent the best economy.

In some questions, the maximum number of jobs that can be created {{curr_block.text_total_end}} is larger than the maximum number that can be created {{curr_block.text_delay_start}}; in others, it is smaller or the same. So, the total number of jobs that can be created over the two periods varies across questions.
